Operating Under Sanctions & Supply Constraints

Iran and parts of the GCC operate under export controls, shipping restrictions and extended lead times for OEM hardware and on-site services. Our maintenance model is designed with these realities in mind, while respecting all applicable laws and regulations.

OEM Warranty Limitations

Standard OEM warranties are usually designed for North America and Europe. In practice, this can mean limited on-site coverage, long response times or no direct service in certain regions. Once the initial 3‚Äì5 year warranty expires, many operators are left without a reliable support structure for critical infrastructure.

TPM as a Stability Layer

Third-Party Maintenance (TPM) fills the gap between OEM warranties and real-world operational needs. Our role is to keep production systems running‚Äîservers, storage and backup platforms‚Äîthrough local engineering, disciplined procedures and a multi-vendor skill set that does not depend on a single OEM.

Spare Parts & Lead Time Management

Sanctions and logistics can extend delivery times for original parts. We design spare-parts strategies around this: identifying critical components, planning local stock, and validating parts in test environments before deployment, so production systems are not waiting on border delays.

Risk Reduction for National Infrastructure

Telecom operators, government agencies and financial institutions cannot afford long outages while waiting for overseas support. Local TPM capability reduces operational risk: incidents are diagnosed and resolved on-site, with escalation to OEMs only when truly necessary.

Compliance & Governance

All activities are planned with attention to legal and regulatory requirements. Our focus is on continuity of service, transparent reporting and clear responsibility lines between the client, the OEM and the TPM provider, so decision-makers can defend their choices in audits and reviews.
